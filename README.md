# Predictors-of-Job-Satisfaction
Employee attrition in the workplace is at an all-time high. This analysis is about understanding the factors that influence job satisfaction to help managers of organisations understand the intrinsic and extrinsic nature of their  employees and develop strategies to facilitate retention.
![image](https://standout-cv.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/07/job-satisfaction-statistics-3.png)
## Dataset

Using a dataset from the Workplace Employment Relations Survey by Stokes and Forth (2008), accessed from the UK Data Service Archive, the question (What are the determinants of job satisfaction?) was addressed. The contents of the dataset are responses from workplaces and employees collected through a cross-sectional survey. It contains around 400 variables (columns) and 22451 cases (observations).

## Measures

==> Independent variables (explanatory).
From the literature, several variables were identified with potential to influence job satisfaction: job security (Cornelißen, 2006; Kim et al., 2023), autonomy (Cornelißen, 2006) and employer-employee relationship (Cornelißen, 2006; Alegre, Mas-Machuca and Berbegal-Miraben, 2016). These articles were chosen because they capture studies on job satisfaction determinants in both private and public sector. 

For this study, relationship (employer-employee), job security, and autonomy (using initiative) were used as the main explanatory variables. These variables were selected because they align with the two-factor theory developed by Friedrick Herzberg, which has been widely deployed by researchers in understanding job satisfaction determinants (Lee et al., 2023).
